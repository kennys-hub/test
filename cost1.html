Cost Analysis 🏡

Detailed Breakdown for a 900 sq ft Adobe Ecotech Container Home in Los Lunas, NM 🌄

Total Cost Overview 💰

The total estimated cost to build this 900 sq ft Adobe Ecotech Container Home in Los Lunas, NM, as of April 2025, is $161,995, or $180 per square foot. This includes all construction, ecotech utilities, landscaping, labor, and additional fees, ensuring a fully off-grid, sustainable homestead.

Construction Costs 🏠

These costs cover the core structure and finishing of the container home, including labor for each component.

  • 📦 Shipping Containers: $14,700
    Two 40 ft containers at $5,750 each ($11,500) and one 20 ft container at $3,200, forming the 900 sq ft structure.
  • 🪨 Foundation: $7,704
    Concrete slab for 800 sq ft (indoor footprint) at $9.63 per square foot, including labor for pouring and finishing.
  • 🔨 Structural Modifications: $6,700
    Includes framing for window/door cuts (200 linear feet at $21 per linear foot, $4,200) and a wrought iron spiral staircase with wooden steps ($2,500), with labor.
  • 🎨 Exterior Finishing (Adobe Santa Fe Style): $12,250
    Adobe plaster and finishing for 900 sq ft at $10 per square foot ($10,000), plus exterior painting at $2.50 per square foot ($2,250), including labor.
  • 🛋️ Interior Finishing: $25,400
    Terracotta tiles (800 sq ft at $8 per square foot, $6,400), drywall (2,400 sq ft at $2.50 per square foot, $6,000), whitewashing walls (2,400 sq ft at $2.50 per square foot, $6,000), kiva fireplace and adobe bench ($3,500), Talavera/zellige tiles ($1,500), and handcrafted furniture/textiles ($2,000), with labor.
  • 🪟 Doors and Windows: $8,200
    10 large windows at $525 each ($5,250), a 6 ft sliding glass door for the balcony ($1,900), and 2 interior doors at $525 each ($1,050), including installation labor.
  • 🏜️ Roofing: $4,050
    Slanted roof with vigas and flat roof for main containers (900 sq ft at $4.50 per square foot), including labor.

Subtotal: $76,204

Ecotech Utilities & Systems 🔋

These systems ensure the homestead is fully off-grid, sustainable, and exceeds NMED standards, with all labor included.

  • 🚽 Septic System (BioMicrobics MicroFAST 0.5 ATU): $14,500
    Includes the ATU tank and mound field ($10,000), plus upgrades: NitrifAST® for nitrogen removal ($1,500), sand filter ($1,000), UV disinfection and carbon filter ($1,200), and smart sensors with sampling port ($800), with installation labor.
  • ☀️ Solar Power (5 kW Array): $10,000
    Located at x=10–30, y=115–135, produces ~20 kWh/day, powers all systems including a 2.4 kW heat pump and UV sterilizers, with installation labor.
  • 💦 Cistern (5,000-gallon): $5,250
    Located at x=30–37, y=25–32, elevated 1–2 ft, includes the cistern and stone pathway ($4,000), plus 50-micron sediment filter ($150), activated carbon filter ($200), UV sterilizer ($300), first-flush diverter ($100), and dry well at x=70–80, y=20–30 ($500), with labor.
  • 🛁 Jacuzzi: $6,000
    Mid-range jacuzzi for 4-6 people, including installation, plumbing, and electrical hookup, powered by the solar array.
  • ♻️ Greywater System: $1,950
    Collects 4,325 gallons/year from showers and laundry, includes a 100-gallon tank ($300), 50-micron filter ($150), piping and subsurface irrigation for cacti rows at x=0–80, y=10–30 ($1,000), and labor ($500).

Subtotal: $37,700
