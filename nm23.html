Property Description 🌵

🌟 Breakthrough: Advanced septic system ensures zero pollution risk to the Rio Grande, despite arroyo connection! 🌟

Tucked into the high desert of Canyon del Rio, Valencia County, NM, this 0.28-acre lot (80' × 150', 12,000 sq ft) at coordinates 34.6911, -106.5575 is Kenny’s vision for sustainable off-grid living. Located in Rio del Oro, Unit 28, Lot 25, Block 27, the lot is a rectangle oriented north-south with the longer axis (150 ft) running north-south (y-axis) and the shorter axis (80 ft) running east-west (x-axis). It has a 3.33% slope from NW (5400 ft) to SE (5390 ft). The plat lines are misaligned on the Valencia County Parcel Map, shifted 10–20 ft southeast, meaning the lot’s true position is 10–20 ft northwest of the map’s depiction (shifted 15 ft NW in diagram).

  • 🌱 Soil: Well-draining sandy loam, ideal for construction but requires careful water management for gardening.
  • 🌊 Arroyo: Camino Rio, located just south of the lot (beyond y=0), 20–30 ft from the true southern boundary (25 ft in diagram), and 5–10 ft below the lot’s elevation (5390 ft at SE), carries rare runoff (~8–10 inches/year). It runs diagonally southwest to northeast and eventually connects to the Rio Grande (10–15 miles away) via regional arroyos during extreme events.
  • 📏 Topography: 10 ft elevation drop (3.33% NW-SE), directing runoff toward the SE corner and into the arroyo below.
  • 🛡️ Zoning & Safety: Zoned RR-1, FEMA Zone X (minimal flood risk). Runoff managed with swales, basins, cacti, and a 5,000-gallon cistern. A survey is recommended to confirm true boundaries.

Utilities & Systems 🔋

Fully off-grid, the homestead features a state-of-the-art septic system and water management to protect the Rio Grande, powered by solar energy. The Camino Rio arroyo’s eventual connection to the Rio Grande requires careful design to meet NMED standards.

  • ☀️ Solar Power: 5 kW array (x=10–30, y=115–135), producing ~20 kWh/day, with a 2-ton, 2.4 kW heat pump for climate control.
  • 🚽 Septic System: BioMicrobics MicroFAST 0.5 ATU tank (8' × 4', x=60–68, y=110–114) with tertiary treatment and smart sensors, ~115–125 ft from the arroyo (beyond y=0, 25 ft south of true southern boundary in diagram).
  • 💧 Mound Field: 10' × 10' (x=60–70, y=100–110), elevated 2–3 ft.
  • 🌵 Vegetative Buffer: Prickly pear and cholla (x=0–80, y=10–30) for natural filtration.
  • 💦 Cistern: 5,000 gallons (x=30–37, y=25–32), elevated 1–2 ft, with a 4" PVC overflow pipe to a dry well (x=70–80, y=20–30).
  • 🌊 Swales & Basins: 80' × 5' swale (x=0–80, y=5–10), primary basin (x=60–70, y=10–20), and secondary basin (x=20–30, y=10–20) for runoff management.
  • 🌱 Garden: 10' × 10' (x=30–40, y=85–95), irrigated with treated effluent.
  • 🚶 Gravel Path: 3' × 16' (x=43–46, y=20–36), connecting home, jacuzzi, and garden.

Why This System is 100% Clean 🌟

The advanced wastewater treatment system and runoff management ensure zero pollution risk to the Rio Grande, despite the Camino Rio arroyo’s eventual connection, meeting and exceeding NMED and Valencia County standards for a sustainable, off-grid homestead.

  • 🧪 Multi-Stage Treatment: BioMicrobics MicroFAST® reduces BOD, TSS, and nitrogen, while NitrifAST® enhances nitrogen removal. A recirculating sand filter eliminates fine solids and phosphorus, UV disinfection removes pathogens, and a granular activated carbon (GAC) filter clears trace organics, ensuring near-zero pollutants.
  • ✅ Exceeds NMED Standards: Effluent achieves <5 mg/L BOD/TSS, <10 mg/L total nitrogen, <1 mg/L phosphorus, and no detectable pathogens, surpassing NMED and Valencia County surface/groundwater requirements (20.6.4 and 20.6.2 NMAC).
  • 🌊 Rio Grande Protection: The arroyo connects to the Rio Grande (10–15 miles away) during extreme events, but effluent reuse via drip irrigation for cacti rows, combined with vegetated buffers (prickly pear, cholla), ensures no impact. The arroyo’s distance (25 ft from true southern boundary, 5–10 ft below) and desert filtration further prevent contamination.
  • 📊 Monitoring: A sampling port allows regular testing of effluent for BOD, TSS, nitrogen, phosphorus, and coliforms, verifying pollutant-free performance and ensuring NMED approval, especially with the Rio Grande connection.
  • 🌞 Sustainability: Powered by a 5 kW solar array with battery backup, using a locally sourced tank, and integrated with swales and cacti rows, the system aligns with eco-friendly goals for Canyon del Rio.

Arroyo & Rio Grande Connection 🌊

The Camino Rio arroyo, located 20–30 ft south of the lot’s true southern boundary (25 ft in diagram) and 5–10 ft below, is an ephemeral desert drainage, dry 99.9% of the time with rare runoff (~8–10 inches/year). While it disperses into the desert for most events, it can connect to the Rio Grande (10–15 miles away) via regional arroyos (S/N Rio del Oro Loops) during extreme runoff (e.g., a 100-year storm), as shown in regional maps.

  • 🛡️ FEMA Zone X: The lot remains in FEMA Zone X (minimal flood risk), with no additional flood mitigation needed beyond the 2 ft elevation and runoff controls (swales, basins).
  • 📏 Setbacks: Structures (e.g., container home at 30–70 ft, retention basin at 10–20 ft) and septic system (~115–125 ft) exceed the 35 ft and 100 ft setbacks from the arroyo, meeting RR-1 zoning and NMAC § 20.7.3 even with the Rio Grande connection.
  • 🚽 Septic Compliance: The advanced BioMicrobics MicroFAST ATU ensures effluent quality (<5 mg/L BOD/TSS), with a soil percolation test and effluent monitoring to meet NMED standards (20.6.4 NMAC) for a “water of the state.”
  • 🛤️ Access: No arroyo crossing is needed for access, eliminating additional permits.
  • ✅ Permitting Rating: 9.5/10, with minor steps (soil test, monitoring) ensuring approval by 2030.
