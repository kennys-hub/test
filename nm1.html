Property Lot Description - Canyon del Rio 🏡

Valencia County Home

Property Description 🌄

This 0.28-acre property in Canyon del Rio, Valencia County, New Mexico, is a sustainable off-grid homestead. Measuring 77’ east-west by 156’ north-south, it features a 2-story site-built home, garage, shed, heat pump, septic system (ATU tank and drain field), cistern, jacuzzi, doghouse, swales, and a gravel path. The property is zoned RR-1 (Rural Residential) and is oriented with the street on the south (y=0), north boundary at y=156, west at x=0, and east at x=77. A 5 kW solar system powers the home, supported by 27 kWh battery storage, making it fully off-grid. 🌞

Home and Utilities 🏠

  • Site-Built Home 🏠: 2-story, 900 sq ft, built with stucco exterior and a flat roof concealed by parapets. Includes an electric stove (5 kW), heat pump indoor air handler, upgraded insulation (R-30 walls, R-50 roof), 32 sq ft south-facing windows, and thermal mass for passive solar heating. Located at columns 3–6, rows 2–4 (15.4’–46.2’, 31.2’–62.4’).
  • Garage 🚗: 20’ × 20’, contains solar batteries (27 kWh), inverter, water pump, vehicle parking, and tools. Located at columns 2–3, rows 2–3 (7.7’–23.1’, 31.2’–46.8’).
  • Shed 🛠️: 6’ × 6’, stores garden tools, cistern accessories, and miscellaneous items. Located at column 2, row 5 (7.7’, - set to (7.7’, 78’).
  • Heat Pump ❄️: 2-ton (24,000 BTU, 2.4 kW), provides cooling and primary heating. Located at column 2, row 4 (7.7’, 62.4’).
  • ATU Tank 🚽: BioMicrobics MicroFAST 0.5 tank (8’ × 4’), part of the septic system. Located at column 6, row 8 (46.2’, 124.8’–128.8’).
  • Drain Field 💧: 10’ × 10’, disperses treated effluent. Located at column 6, row 7 (46.2’, 109.2’–119.2’).
  • Cistern 💦: 2,000-gallon rainwater cistern (10’ × 7.5’), collects runoff. Located at column 6, row 2 (46.2’, 31.2’).
  • Jacuzzi 🛁: 7’ × 7’, for relaxation. Located at column 4, row 5 (30.8’, 78’).
  • Doghouse 🐶: 3’ × 3’, pet shelter. Located at column 2, row 10 (7.7’, 140.4’–156’).
  • Swales 🌊: Runoff mitigations (50’ × 5’), manage water flow. Located at columns 2–6, row 2 (7.7’–46.2’, 15.6’–31.2’).
  • Path 🚶: Gravel path to the jacuzzi (3’ × 10’), connects the home to the jacuzzi. Located at columns 4–6, row 5 (30.8’–46.2’, 78’).
  • Solar System ☀️: 5 kW, roof-mounted on the home, produces ~20 kWh/day.

Applicable Laws 📜

Valencia County RR-1 Zoning (Article 5, Section 154.071)

  • Front Setback: 25’ from the street (y=0).
  • Side Setback: 10’ from side property lines (x=0, x=77).
  • Rear Setback: 10’ from the rear property line (y=156).
  • Lot Coverage: Maximum 35% for all structures.

Canyon del Rio ACC Manual (Adopted October 29, 1998)

  • Front Setback: 20’ from the front lot line (Section 5-2-1).
  • Side Setback: 5’ from side lot lines (10’ if a street right-of-way on a corner lot) (Section 5-2-2).
  • Rear Setback: 15’ from the rear lot line (Section 5-2-3).
  • Drainage Easement Setback: 35’ from any lot line abutting a drainage easement (Section 5-2-4).
  • Minimum Square Footage: 800 sq ft for the main structure (Section 5-3-1).
  • Minimum Width: 24’ for the main structure (Section 5-3-2).
  • Garage Requirement: At least a one-car garage (250 sq ft) (Section 5-3-3).
  • Exterior Design: Stucco, earthtone colors, flat roof concealed by parapets (Sections 5-4-1 to 5-4-5).
  • Utilities: Electric lines must be buried underground (Section 5-7).
  • Drainage: A drainage plan may be required due to the nearby arroyo (Section 5-8).

NMAC § 20.7.3 (Liquid Waste Treatment and Disposal)

  • ATU Tank and Drain Field Setbacks:
    • 5’ from property lines.
    • 10’ from buildings.
    • 4’ from non-paved surfaces (e.g., paths).
    • 50’ from water storage systems (e.g., cistern).
