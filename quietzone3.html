Sound Path Analysis - Canyon del Rio

Step 1: Establish Baseline and Sound Source

The quarry is located 1.10 km north of the plat near Los Lunas, NM, with elevations ranging from 5300 ft to 5460 ft, generating 22-33 dBA at that distance. This aligns with typical quarry noise levels of 80-100 dB at the source, attenuating with distance. Using the inverse square law, sound intensity decreases by 6 dB per doubling of distance. At 1.10 km (1100 m):

  • Assume quarry noise at source: ~90 dB (mid-range for quarry activity).
  • Distance attenuation: From 1 m to 1100 m, distance doubles ~10 times (log₂(1100) ≈ 10), yielding 6 dB × 10 = 60 dB.
  • Additional atmospheric absorption (at 1 kHz, ~0.005 dB/m in semi-arid conditions): 1100 m × 0.005 = 5.5 dB.
  • Total attenuation: 60 + 5.5 = 65.5 dB.
  • Resulting noise at 1.10 km: 90 - 65.5 = 24.5 dBA, within the range of 22-33 dBA. I’ll use 25 dBA as the baseline at the starting point (elevation ~5460 ft, with lower areas at 5300 ft).

Step 2: Analyze the Sound Path with Topographic Effects

The green path on the map traces the sound route from the quarry (5460 ft, with lower areas at 5300 ft) to the plat (5320 ft), considering the plat’s 3.33% slope (10 ft drop from NW at 5,400 ft to SE at 5,390 ft over 150 ft). The path includes a northern ridge, two hills, three arroyos, and a final descent to the plat. Segment 1: Descent into Lull and Arroyo (Quarry to Base of Hill 1)

Topography: The path starts at the quarry (~5460 ft, with lower areas at 5300 ft) and descends to a lull near an arroyo (~5380-5400 ft, 5-10 ft below), with a northern ridge (~5440-5460 ft) providing initial shielding.

Sound Dynamics:

  • Ridge Shielding: The northern ridge reduces noise by 3 dB (conservative estimate due to quarry’s lower areas at 5300 ft reducing effective height).
  • Vegetation: Sparse vegetation (sagebrush, cholla) adds ~1 dB absorption.
  • Ground Effect: Sandy loam soil absorbs ~1 dB.

dB Calculation:

  • Starting noise: 25 dBA.
  • Reduction: 3 (ridge) + 1 (vegetation) + 1 (ground) = 5 dB.
  • Noise at lull/arroyo base: 25 - 5 = 20 dBA.

Segment 2: Ascent of Hill 1

Topography: Hill 1 rises to ~5400-5440 ft (10-20 ft gain, 10-15% slope).

Sound Dynamics:

  • Elevation Increase: Slope reduces noise by ~3 dB (conservative estimate for diffraction over low-frequency quarry noise).
  • Vegetation: Piñon pines and juniper absorb ~1 dB.

dB Calculation:

  • Noise at base: 20 dBA.
  • Reduction: 3 (hill) + 1 (vegetation) = 4 dB.
  • Noise at Hill 1 peak: 20 - 4 = 16 dBA.

Segment 3: Descent into Lull Over Three Arroyos

Topography: The path descends to a lull (~5380-5400 ft), crossing three arroyos as specified.

Sound Dynamics:

  • Arroyo Absorption: Each arroyo reduces noise by ~2 dB (total 6 dB), reflecting conservative absorption in gravel-lined arroyos.
  • Lull Effect: Minimal reduction, ~1 dB.
  • Ambient Noise: Sheltered areas align with 20-25 dBA, but calculations remain independent for now.

dB Calculation:

  • Noise at Hill 1 peak: 16 dBA.
  • Reduction: 6 (arroyos) + 1 (lull) = 7 dB.
  • Noise after arroyos: 16 - 7 = 9 dBA.

Segment 4: Ascent of Hill 2

Topography: Hill 2 rises to ~5400 ft (20-30 ft gain).

Sound Dynamics:

  • Elevation Increase: Slope reduces noise by ~3 dB.
  • Vegetation: Cholla and yucca absorb ~1 dB.

  • Noise at Hill 2 peak: 9 - 4 = 5 dBA.

Segment 5: Descent to Your Plat

Topography: The plat is at ~5320 ft, with a 3.33% slope (10 ft drop from NW at 5,400 ft to SE at 5,390 ft over 150 ft). The due north view shows the quarry’s elevation (~5460 ft, with lower areas at 5300 ft), adding 1-2 dB from inversions, and wind (5-10 mph northerly) adds 1-2 dB at 05:15 AM MDT.

Sound Dynamics:

  • Environmental Adjustment: Inversions (1-2 dB) and wind (1-2 dB) increase noise by 2-4 dB.
  • Berm Effect: A 6-8 ft berm reduces noise by 5-7 dB; a 10-12 ft berm reduces by 8-10 dB.
  • Natural Sounds: Fountain produces 15-20 dBA, leaves add 10-15 dBA, combining to ~18 dBA.

dB Calculation:

  • Noise at Hill 2 peak: 5 dBA.
  • Environmental increase: 2-4 dB.
  • Noise at plat without berm: 5 + 2-4 = 7-9 dBA.
  • With 6-8 ft berm (5-7 dB): 7-9 - 5-7 = 2-4 dBA.
  • With 10-12 ft berm (8-10 dB): 7-9 - 8-10 = -1-1 dBA (essentially inaudible).
  • Adjusted for natural sounds: 2-4 dBA (quarry) + 18 dBA (natural) = ~18 dBA (natural sounds dominate).

Segment 6: Final Consideration at Plat

Topography: The plat’s 3.33% slope and proximity to the Camino Rio arroyo (~25 ft southeast of y=0) were considered in the berm placement (x=15-55, y=130), ensuring effective noise reduction.

Sound Dynamics:

  • Final Noise: Quarry noise is minimal (2-4 dBA with 6-8 ft berm), and total noise is dominated by natural sounds at ~18 dBA.
