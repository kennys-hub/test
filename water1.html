100% Clean Water Filtration in the Southwest

Sustainable water management for desert living in the Rio del Oro area

Property Overview

Location

This 0.28-acre lot is located in Canyon del Rio, Valencia County, New Mexico, with coordinates at 34.6911, -106.5575. With a 3.33% slope and sandy loam soil, it’s ideal for sustainable building and wastewater management.

Topography & Water Flow

The land features a 10 ft elevation drop, directing runoff toward an arroyo. The arroyo enters at (0,75) at a 35° angle and exits at (80,35) at a 30° angle. The site’s design ensures minimal flood risk with FEMA Zone X designation.

Water Filtration & Wastewater Treatment

Swale & Retention Basins

Natural swales and retention basins manage water runoff and help filter contaminants, allowing for a more eco-friendly and efficient system for collecting and managing stormwater.

Cistern for Water Storage

A 5,000-gallon cistern stores harvested rainwater for irrigation, providing an additional sustainable water source for the property while minimizing dependency on external water supplies.

Advanced Septic & Filtration System

BioMicrobics MicroFAST® Septic System

High-efficiency septic treatment system designed to treat wastewater through aeration, filtering, and disinfection, minimizing environmental impact while ensuring safety for the Rio Grande ecosystem.

Elevated Mound System

An elevated mound system distributes treated effluent through sand and gravel layers, providing further filtration before water enters the environment, reducing the risk of contaminants.

Solar Powered and Energy Efficient

Solar Array

The 5kW solar array powers all systems on-site, including the MicroFAST® septic system’s aeration unit and UV disinfection system, ensuring sustainable energy use and reducing your carbon footprint.

UV Disinfection

A UV disinfection unit ensures that treated water is free of harmful pathogens, providing a final level of safety before effluent is released into the arroyo, safeguarding both the local environment and the Rio Grande.

Water Treatment Flow

1. Wastewater is first treated by the MicroFAST® system.

2. Treated water flows through an elevated mound system and gravel filter, polishing the effluent.

3. A UV disinfection unit provides the final step of purification before water enters the arroyo.
