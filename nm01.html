Property Description 🌵

🌟 Breakthrough: Advanced septic system ensures zero pollution risk to the Rio Grande! 🌟

Tucked into the high desert of Canyon del Rio, Valencia County, NM, this 0.28-acre lot (80' × 150', 12,000 sq ft) at coordinates 34.6911, -106.5575 is Kenny’s vision for sustainable off-grid living. Located in Rio del Oro, Unit 28, Lot 25, Block 27, the lot is a diagonal rectangle oriented southwest to northeast with a 3.33% slope from north (5400 ft) to south (5390 ft).

  • 🌱 Soil: Well-draining sandy loam, ideal for construction but requires careful water management for gardening.
  • 🌊 Arroyo: Positioned just southeast of the lot, running parallel to the SE boundary (x=80), carrying rare runoff (~8–10 inches/year) to the Rio Grande via Camino Rio.
  • 📏 Topography: 10 ft elevation drop (3.33% N-S, 5.88% diagonal), directing runoff toward the arroyo.
  • 🛡️ Zoning & Safety: Zoned RR-1, FEMA Zone X (minimal flood risk). Runoff managed with swales, cacti, and a 5,000-gallon cistern.

Utilities & Systems 🔋

Fully off-grid, the homestead features a state-of-the-art septic system and water management to protect the Rio Grande, powered by solar energy.

  • ☀️ Solar Power: 5 kW array (x=30–50, y=10–30), producing ~20 kWh/day, with a 2-ton, 2.4 kW heat pump for climate control.
  • 🚽 Septic System: BioMicrobics MicroFAST 0.5 ATU tank (8' × 4', x=0–8, y=120–124) with tertiary treatment and smart sensors, over 100 ft from the arroyo.
  • 💧 Mound Field: 10' × 10' (x=0–10, y=110–120), elevated 2–3 ft, with a 15' × 15' berm (y=108–123) and 30' × 8' swale (y=125–133).
  • 🪨 Gravel Filter: 10' × 10' (x=40–50, y=125–135) to catch overflow.
  • 🌵 Vegetative Buffer: Prickly pear and cholla (x=0–80, y=35–120) for natural filtration.
  • 💦 Cistern: 5,000 gallons (x=40–47, y=40–47), elevated 1–2 ft, with a 4" PVC overflow pipe to a dry well (x=70–80, y=30–40).
  • 🌊 Swales & Basins: 80' × 5' swale (x=0–80, y=10–15), primary basin (x=65–75, y=15–25), and secondary basin (x=20–30, y=20–30) for runoff management.
  • 🌱 Garden: 10' × 10' (x=40–50, y=35–45), irrigated with treated effluent.
  • 🚶 Gravel Path: 3' × 16' (x=45–48, y=35–51), connecting home, jacuzzi, and garden.

Why This System is 100% Clean 🌟

The advanced wastewater treatment system ensures zero pollution risk to the Rio Grande, meeting and exceeding NMED and Valencia County standards for a sustainable, off-grid homestead.

  • 🧪 Multi-Stage Treatment: BioMicrobics MicroFAST® reduces BOD, TSS, and nitrogen, while NitrifAST® enhances nitrogen removal. A recirculating sand filter eliminates fine solids and phosphorus, UV disinfection removes pathogens, and a granular activated carbon (GAC) filter clears trace organics, ensuring near-zero pollutants.
  • ✅ Exceeds NMED Standards: Effluent achieves <5 mg/L BOD/TSS, <10 mg/L total nitrogen, <1 mg/L phosphorus, and no detectable pathogens, surpassing NMED and Valencia County surface/groundwater requirements (20.6.4 and 20.6.2 NMAC).
  • 🌊 Rio Grande Protection: Options for effluent reuse via drip irrigation for cacti rows or discharge through a gravel filter and swale, combined with vegetated buffers (prickly pear, cholla), prevent any impact on the arroyo or Rio Grande.
  • 📊 Monitoring: A sampling port allows regular testing of effluent for BOD, TSS, nitrogen, phosphorus, and coliforms, verifying pollutant-free performance and ensuring NMED/Valencia County approval.
  • 🌞 Sustainability: Powered by a 5 kW solar array with battery backup, using a locally sourced tank, and integrated with swales and cacti rows, the system aligns with eco-friendly goals for Canyon del Rio.

Summary 💡

🌟 Kenny’s vision: Sustainable, Safe, Smart, Secluded 🌟

Kenny’s 900 sq ft container home in Canyon del Rio, set for a 2030 build, is a model of sustainable living on a 0.28-acre lot (80' × 150'). Zoned RR-1 and in FEMA Zone X, it features a state-of-the-art septic system with tertiary treatment, a mound field, gravel filter, and vegetative buffers, ensuring zero pollution to the Rio Grande. The corrected lot position places it above the arroyo, ensuring the septic system meets the 100 ft setback requirement, facilitating NMED approval. Solar power, a 5,000-gallon cistern, swales, and cacti manage the arid desert’s resources. With a buildable area of 3,150 sq ft (~26.25%) and 14.99% lot coverage, it complies with RR-1 zoning. Accessible to Belen (18 miles, 25–30 minutes) and Albuquerque (~40 minutes), this off-grid homestead blends seclusion with convenience, respecting the desert’s natural flow.
