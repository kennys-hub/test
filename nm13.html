Property Description 🌄

🌟 Breakthrough: The arid arroyo doesn’t connect to any waterways, ensuring zero pollution risk from the septic system and boosting build confidence! 🌟

Nestled in the arid desert of Canyon del Rio, Valencia County, New Mexico, this 0.28-acre lot (80' × 150', 12,000 sq ft) is perfect for sustainable off-grid living. Located in Rio del Oro, Unit 28, Lot 25, Block 27, at coordinates -106.547295, 34.713121, the lot is a diagonal rectangle oriented southwest to northeast. Access to Belen starts with a 1-2 mile dirt road to Rio del Oro Loop South, a 5-mile gravel road, then the paved Manzano Expressway for about 11 miles to NM-47, reaching Belen’s amenities like Walmart, schools, and the train in 20-25 minutes (17.9 miles total). An arid arroyo, dry except during rare rain events (~8–10 inches annually), enters at the western boundary (x=0, y=75) at a 35-degree angle and exits at the southeast (x=80, y=35) at a 30-degree angle, dispersing runoff into the desert without linking to rivers or streams. The land slopes from north (5400 ft elevation) to south (5390 ft) at 3.33% north-south and 5.88% diagonally, directing minimal runoff. Zoned RR-1 and in FEMA Zone X (low flood risk), the lot’s isolated arroyo slashes septic pollution concerns, with swales, cacti, and a cistern managing runoff. Ideal for Kenny, this rural retreat offers privacy, energy independence, and harmony with the desert landscape. 🏜️

The Container Home and Utilities 🏠

The centerpiece is a solar-powered, 900 sq ft container home built from three shipping containers (two 40 ft × 8 ft on the ground, one 20 ft × 8 ft on top). Designed for desert sustainability, it thrives in FEMA Zone X’s low flood risk, with the arroyo’s isolation ensuring no waterway pollution from the septic system. A mound system for the drain field enhances treatment, virtually guaranteeing a reduced arroyo setback with NMED approval.

  • 🏠Container Home: 900 sq ft (40' × 16' bottom footprint, 20' × 8' top). Two 40 ft containers side by side (640 sq ft) and one 20 ft on top (160 sq ft), plus a 100 sq ft deck. Features spray foam insulation (R-13), 32 sq ft windows, external staircase. Positioned at x=40 to x=56, y=50 to y=90, elevated 2 ft to manage rare arroyo runoff.
  • 🚗Garage: 20’ × 12’ garage at x=40 to x=52, y=100 to y=120, for vehicle storage and workshop space.
  • 🛠️Shed: 8’ × 6’ shed at x=60 to x=66, y=100 to y=108, for additional storage.
  • ❄️Heat Pump: 2-ton system (2.4 kW) for efficient desert climate control.
  • 🚽ATU Tank: BioMicrobics MicroFAST 0.5 tank (8’ × 4’) at x=50 to x=58, y=126 to y=130, downslope for gravity-assisted waste flow to a pump for the mound system.
  • 💧Drain Field (Mound System): 10’ × 10’ mound at x=55 to x=65, y=110 to y=120, elevated 2–3 ft to enhance effluent treatment in desert soils, 55.9 ft from the isolated arroyo, set to secure a 50-ft setback with NMED approval.
  • 💦Cistern: 5,000-gallon cistern at x=60 to x=70, y=40 to y=47, elevated 1–2 ft with an overflow pipe to the arroyo (x=80, y=35), collecting runoff via a diversion pipe, supported by cacti rows in this arid climate.
  • 🛁Jacuzzi: 6’ × 6’ outdoor jacuzzi at x=50 to x=56, y=30 to y=36, elevated 2 ft on a gravel pad with a protective berm for desert dust control.
  • 🐶Doghouse: 3’ × 3’ doghouse at x=50 to x=53, y=45 to y=48, on stable ground for pet comfort.
  • 🌊Swales: 80' × 5' swale along the southern boundary at x=0 to x=80, y=5 to y=10, to capture rare arroyo runoff in Zone X.
  • 🌊Retention Basin: 10’ × 10’ basin at x=60 to x=70, y=10 to y=20, to hold excess runoff, with a diversion pipe to the cistern.
  • 🚶Gravel Path: 3’ × 20’ permeable gravel path at x=50 to x=53, y=30 to y=50, connecting home, jacuzzi, and garden, suited for desert drainage.
  • 🌱Garden: 10’ × 10’ garden at x=40 to x=50, y=30 to y=40, south-facing for desert sunlight access.
  • 🌵Cacti Rows: Prickly pear rows at x=35 to x=70, y=60 and y=50, to slow and guide arroyo runoff toward the cistern, with cholla along a channel (x=59 to x=71, y=40 to y=50) for water flow in this arid environment.
  • ☀️Solar Power: 5 kW solar array at x=40 to x=60, y=0 to y=20, south-facing to maximize desert sun exposure, producing ~20 kWh/day, safe from the isolated arroyo’s rare runoff.
