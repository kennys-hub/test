Property Description 🌄

Nestled in the stunning Canyon del Rio, Valencia County, New Mexico, this 0.28-acre lot (measuring 80' × 150' or 12,000 sq ft) offers the perfect opportunity for sustainable living. The lot is a diagonal rectangle oriented from southwest to northeast, with an arroyo running west to southeast along the western boundary. The land slopes down from north (higher elevation) to south (lower elevation) into the arroyo, directing water flow naturally. The property is designed for off-grid living with all necessary utilities and is located in an ideal rural setting for peace, privacy, and a connection to nature. This land is zoned RR-1 and is perfectly suited for Kenny—looking for a blend of comfort, energy independence, and natural beauty. 🏜️

Lot Orientation and Dimensions 📐

The lot’s longer axis (150 ft) runs from southwest (lower left) to northeast (upper right), and the shorter axis (80 ft) runs perpendicular, from northwest to southeast. We’ll define the lot’s bounding box in a rotated coordinate system where:

  • 📏The longer axis (150 ft) is the "y-axis" (y=0 at the southwest corner, y=150 at the northeast corner).
  • 📏The shorter axis (80 ft) is the "x-axis" (x=0 at the northwest edge, x=80 at the southeast edge).
  • 🌊The arroyo runs along the western boundary (approximately x=0), sloping from west to southeast, with the land sloping down from north to south into the arroyo.

The coordinates from the parcel map (106.546671, 34.712766 degrees) place this in Canyon del Rio, Valencia County. 🏜️

The Home and Utilities 🏠

The centerpiece of this property is a solar-powered, two-story, 900 sq ft container home, built with adobe and stucco materials. Designed with eco-conscious principles, the home minimizes energy use while maximizing comfort. The drain field has been repositioned to prevent runoff into the arroyo, ensuring compliance and environmental safety.

  • 🏠Two-Story Home: The 900 sq ft container home offers a sustainable, off-grid living experience. Features include thermal mass construction, R-30/R-50 insulation, and 32 sq ft of windows. The home is powered by solar energy and includes an electric stove, heat pump, and ample natural light. Positioned at x=37 to x=67, y=90 to y=120 for stability on the higher northeastern slope.
  • 🚗Garage: A 20' × 20' garage at x=37 to x=57, y=65 to y=85, with ample space for solar batteries, an inverter, a water pump, tools, and parking.
  • 🛠️Shed: A 6’ × 6’ storage shed at x=61 to x=67, y=65 to y=71, for gardening tools and cistern equipment.
  • ❄️Heat Pump: A 2-ton system (2.4 kW) ensures efficient climate control.
  • 🚽ATU Tank: A BioMicrobics MicroFAST 0.5 tank (8’ × 4’) at x=35 to x=43, y=100 to y=104, downslope for gravity-assisted waste flow.
  • 💧Drain Field: A 10’ × 10’ drain field at x=40 to x=50, y=110 to y=120, moved higher on the slope to prevent runoff into the arroyo.
  • 💦Cistern: A 2,000-gallon cistern at x=60 to x=70, y=125 to y=132.5, near the high northeastern end to avoid pooling.
  • 🛁Jacuzzi: A 7’ × 7’ jacuzzi at x=55 to x=62, y=25 to y=32, near the southwestern front for relaxation.
  • 🐶Doghouse: A 3’ × 3’ doghouse at x=67 to x=70, y=90 to y=93, for pet comfort on stable ground.
  • 🌊Swales: Two swales (50' × 5' each) along the northwestern edge at x=35 to x=40, y=60 to y=110 and y=25 to y=75, to capture runoff and protect the arroyo.
  • 🚶Path: A 3’ × 10’ gravel path at x=55 to x=58, y=32 to y=42, connecting the home to the jacuzzi.
  • ☀️Solar Power: A 5 kW solar array on the roof, south-facing to maximize sun exposure, producing around 20 kWh of energy per day.
