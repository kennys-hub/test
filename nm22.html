Property Description 🌵

🌟 Breakthrough: Arroyo below lot ensures zero flood risk and no pollution to the Rio Grande! 🌟

Tucked into the high desert of Canyon del Rio, Valencia County, NM, this 0.28-acre lot (80' × 150', 12,000 sq ft) at coordinates 34.6911, -106.5575 is Kenny’s vision for sustainable off-grid living. Located in Rio del Oro, Unit 28, Lot 25, Block 27, the lot is a rectangle oriented southwest to northeast with a 3.33% slope from NW (5400 ft) to SE (5390 ft). The plat lines are misaligned on the Valencia County Parcel Map, shifted 10–20 ft southeast, meaning the lot’s true position is 10–20 ft northwest of the map’s depiction.

  • 🌱 Soil: Well-draining sandy loam, ideal for construction but requires careful water management for gardening.
  • 🌊 Arroyo: Located just southeast of the lot (beyond x=80), 20–30 ft from the true SE corner, and 5–10 ft below the lot’s elevation (5390 ft at SE), carrying rare runoff (~8–10 inches/year) to the Rio Grande via regional tributaries.
  • 📏 Topography: 10 ft elevation drop (3.33% NW-SE), directing runoff toward the SE corner and into the arroyo below.
  • 🛡️ Zoning & Safety: Zoned RR-1, FEMA Zone X (minimal flood risk). Runoff managed with swales, basins, cacti, and a 5,000-gallon cistern. A survey is recommended to confirm true boundaries.

Utilities & Systems 🔋

Fully off-grid, the homestead features a state-of-the-art septic system and water management to protect the Rio Grande, powered by solar energy.

  • ☀️ Solar Power: 5 kW array (x=30–50, y=10–30), producing ~20 kWh/day, with a 2-ton, 2.4 kW heat pump for climate control.
  • 🚽 Septic System: BioMicrobics MicroFAST 0.5 ATU tank (8' × 4', x=0–8, y=120–124) with tertiary treatment and smart sensors, ~124–134 ft from the arroyo (beyond x=80).
  • 💧 Mound Field: 10' × 10' (x=0–10, y=110–120), elevated 2–3 ft.
  • 🌵 Vegetative Buffer: Prickly pear and cholla (x=0–80, y=35–80) for natural filtration.
  • 💦 Cistern: 5,000 gallons (x=40–47, y=40–47), elevated 1–2 ft, with a 4" PVC overflow pipe to a dry well (x=70–80, y=30–40).
  • 🌊 Swales & Basins: 80' × 5' swale (x=0–80, y=10–15), primary basin (x=60–70, y=15–25), and secondary basin (x=20–30, y=20–30) for runoff management.
  • 🌱 Garden: 10' × 10' (x=40–50, y=120–130), irrigated with treated effluent.
  • 🚶 Gravel Path: 3' × 16' (x=45–48, y=35–51), connecting home, jacuzzi, and garden.

Why This System is 100% Clean 🌟

The advanced wastewater treatment system and runoff management ensure zero pollution risk to the Rio Grande, meeting and exceeding NMED and Valencia County standards for a sustainable, off-grid homestead.

  • 🧪 Multi-Stage Treatment: BioMicrobics MicroFAST® reduces BOD, TSS, and nitrogen, while NitrifAST® enhances nitrogen removal. A recirculating sand filter eliminates fine solids and phosphorus, UV disinfection removes pathogens, and a granular activated carbon (GAC) filter clears trace organics, ensuring near-zero pollutants.
  • ✅ Exceeds NMED Standards: Effluent achieves <5 mg/L BOD/TSS, <10 mg/L total nitrogen, <1 mg/L phosphorus, and no detectable pathogens, surpassing NMED and Valencia County surface/groundwater requirements (20.6.4 and 20.6.2 NMAC).
  • 🌊 Rio Grande Protection: Options for effluent reuse via drip irrigation for cacti rows or discharge through swales and basins, combined with vegetated buffers (prickly pear, cholla), prevent any impact on the arroyo (20–30 ft from true SE corner, 5–10 ft below) or Rio Grande (10–15 miles away).
  • 📊 Monitoring: A sampling port allows regular testing of effluent for BOD, TSS, nitrogen, phosphorus, and coliforms, verifying pollutant-free performance and ensuring NMED/Valencia County approval.
  • 🌞 Sustainability: Powered by a 5 kW solar array with battery backup, using a locally sourced tank, and integrated with swales and cacti rows, the system aligns with eco-friendly goals for Canyon del Rio.

Summary 💡

🌟 Kenny’s vision: Sustainable, Safe, Smart, Secluded 🌟

Kenny’s 800 sq ft container home in Canyon del Rio, set for a 2030 build, is a model of sustainable living on a 0.28-acre lot (80' × 150'). Zoned RR-1 and in FEMA Zone X, it features a state-of-the-art septic system, solar power, a 5,000-gallon cistern, swales, basins, and cacti for runoff management, ensuring zero pollution to the Rio Grande (10–15 miles away). The lot’s true position is 10–20 ft NW of the map’s depiction, with the arroyo 20–30 ft from the true SE corner and 5–10 ft below, eliminating arroyo setbacks. With a buildable area of 5,250 sq ft (~43.75%) and 8.84% lot coverage (1,061 sq ft), it complies with RR-1 zoning. Accessible to Belen (18 miles, 25–30 minutes) and Albuquerque (~40 minutes), this off-grid homestead blends seclusion with convenience, respecting the desert’s natural flow.
